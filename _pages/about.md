The **Chand Quantum Sensing Lab** develops quantum sensors built from atomic-scale
spin defects and uses them to image phenomena that are invisible to conventional
probes. We work primarily with **nitrogen-vacancy (NV) centers in diamond** and
**spin defects in hexagonal boron nitride (V$_B^-$)**, combining widefield optical
magnetometry, large-scale multiplexed readout, and cryogenic measurement to study
**2D quantum materials** — van der Waals magnets, correlated systems, and
superconductors.

Our goal is to turn quantum sensing into a microscope: spatially resolved,
quantitative maps of magnetism, current, and spin noise across a sample, taken
under the temperature and field conditions where the interesting physics lives.

We are a new group launching in the
[Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering](https://engineering.buffalo.edu/electrical.html)
at the University at Buffalo. **We are actively recruiting** PhD students and
postdocs — see the [Join Us]({{ "/join/" | relative_url }}) page.

#### What we do
- **Multiplexed NV microscopy** — spin-to-charge conversion readout of large NV arrays for high-throughput, parallel sensing.
- **NV & V$_B^-$ sensing of 2D quantum materials** — probing magnetic order, domain dynamics, and critical behavior in van der Waals systems (CrSBr, CrI₃, Fe₃GaTe₂, NiPS₃, FePS₃).
- **Quantum-enhanced & noise spectroscopy** — spatially resolved measurements of spin and charge fluctuations that connect directly to microscopic theory.

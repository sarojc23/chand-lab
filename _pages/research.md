Our research lies at the intersection of **condensed matter physics**, **quantum
optics**, and **quantum information science**. We develop and apply experimental
techniques to probe and control strongly correlated quantum systems, with a focus
on **nitrogen-vacancy (NV) centers in diamond**, **spin defects in hexagonal boron
nitride**, and **two-dimensional quantum materials**.

A central theme is **leveraging spatial and temporal correlations** among
individual quantum defects to uncover emergent dynamics in complex many-body
systems — bridging theoretical models with high-precision measurement to explore
quantum coherence and decoherence, spin–phonon interactions, collective spin
dynamics, and quasiparticle physics in 2D materials.

---

## 1 · Multiplexed NV microscopy

Single-defect measurements are powerful but slow. We develop **massively parallel
readout** of NV center arrays using spin-to-charge conversion, so that hundreds to
thousands of sensors can be interrogated simultaneously. Inspired by optical
tweezer–array techniques from the neutral-atom community, this enables
**simultaneous measurement of many individual NV centers** — a step toward
**parallel correlation measurements** and **scalable covariance magnetometry**.
Combined with structured illumination (spatial light modulators and DMD-based
addressing at the Fourier plane), the platform becomes a true high-throughput
quantum imaging instrument.

*Keywords: spin-to-charge conversion, widefield magnetometry, SLM/DMD beam shaping, covariance magnetometry.*

---

## 2 · Sensing 2D quantum materials

Van der Waals magnets and correlated 2D systems host physics — atomically thin
magnetic order, current-carrying edge states, fluctuating spin textures — that is
difficult to access with bulk probes. We bring the sensor to within nanometers of
the sample and **image magnetism and noise directly**, including with
**V$_B^-$ defects in hexagonal boron nitride**, which can be placed in intimate
contact with a target flake.

This thrust builds on extensive prior work on the electronic and optical
properties of 2D materials — exciton dynamics and transport in transition-metal
dichalcogenides, tunability through gating, strain, and layer stacking, and
light–matter interactions relevant to photonics and valleytronics.

*Keywords: van der Waals magnets, hBN spin defects, magnetic imaging, exciton dynamics, critical phenomena.*

---

## 3 · Quantum-enhanced sensing & noise spectroscopy

Beyond static maps, the fluctuations a sensor picks up carry the fingerprint of
microscopic dynamics. We develop **spatially resolved noise spectroscopy**:
relaxometry and decoherence-based protocols that measure spin and charge
fluctuations as a function of position, frequency, temperature, and field. These
observables connect directly to microscopic theory and reach quantities that
sample-averaged measurements cannot isolate.

*Keywords: relaxometry, decoherence spectroscopy, fluctuation imaging, quantum-enhanced metrology.*

---

### Future directions & emerging platforms

We are interested in extending these methods to new quantum defect systems with
robust spin properties and long coherence times, including **boron vacancies in
hexagonal boron nitride (hBN)** and **divacancies in silicon carbide (SiC)**.
These open opportunities to study spin–phonon coupling, quantum coherence, and
correlated many-body dynamics in previously inaccessible material systems, and to
build interdisciplinary testbeds at the interface of condensed matter, quantum
optics, and quantum information science.

### Infrastructure

The lab is built around a low-vibration **closed-cycle optical cryostat with an
integrated vector magnet**, paired with confocal and widefield optical setups,
microwave spin control, and 2D-materials fabrication capability through close
collaboration with materials and device groups at UB.
